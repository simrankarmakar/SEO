How Google Ranking Works

Core ranking factors: relevance, authority, and user experience.

Introduction

Google’s goal is to show the most relevant, trustworthy, and useful results for each query. Ranking is driven by hundreds of signals, but they group roughly into three areas: relevance, authority, and user experience.

Relevance

Relevance means how well a page matches what the user is looking for. Google looks at:

  • Keywords: Where and how keywords appear (title, headings, body, URL). Not just density—context and intent matter.
  • Topic: Whether the page is about the same topic as the query. Topical clusters and related terms help.
  • Search intent: Whether the page type (product, guide, brand) matches what the user wants (buy, learn, go to a site).

On-page SEO (title tags, meta descriptions, headings, content) is mainly about relevance.

Authority

Authority is how much Google trusts the page and the site. Key signals:

  • Backlinks: Links from other sites. Quality and relevance of linking pages matter more than raw count.
  • Domain authority: Overall strength of the domain (often estimated by tools like Ahrefs DR or Moz DA).
  • E-E-A-T: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness. Google uses this for YMYL (Your Money Your Life) and informational content.

Off-page SEO (link building, brand) builds authority.

User Experience

Google uses behavioral and technical signals to infer whether users are satisfied:

  • Core Web Vitals: LCP (loading), FID/INP (interactivity), CLS (visual stability).
  • Mobile friendliness: Responsive design, readable text, tap targets.
  • Engagement: Click-through rate (CTR), bounce rate, time on page, pogo-sticking. These are inferred, not directly sent to Google.

Technical SEO and UX improvements support these signals.

Putting It Together

To rank well, you need:

  1. Relevant content that matches the query and intent (on-page SEO).
  2. Authority through links and trust (off-page SEO).
  3. Good UX and technical health (Core Web Vitals, mobile, structure).
