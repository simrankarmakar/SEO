Keyword Research

How to find keywords, understand search intent, and cluster keywords for better rankings and content strategy.

How to Find Keywords

Keyword research is the foundation of SEO. You need to discover what people search for and how competitive those terms are.

Methods

  • Seed keywords: Start with your product, service, or topic. Use Google Autocomplete, "People also ask", and "Related searches" at the bottom of SERPs.
  • Keyword tools: Use tools like Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs, SEMrush, or Ubersuggest to get search volume, difficulty, and related keywords.
  • Competitor analysis: See which keywords your competitors rank for. Tools like Ahrefs Site Explorer or SEMrush Organic Research show their top pages and keywords.
  • Content gaps: Find topics your competitors cover that you don’t, or queries where no one fully satisfies intent.

Search Intent Types

Google ranks pages that match search intent. Align your content and page type with the intent behind the keyword.

Informational

User wants to learn. Queries like "how does SEO work", "what is keyword research". Best served by blog posts, guides, and FAQs.

Navigational

User wants to reach a specific site or page. Queries like "Nykaa login", "Amazon India". Brand pages and homepage are key.

Transactional

User wants to buy or take action. Queries like "buy makeup online", "best laptop under 50000". Product pages, category pages, and landing pages.

Some keywords are mixed intent (e.g. "best moisturizer" can be research or buy). Use SERP analysis: if top results are product pages, treat it as transactional; if guides, informational.

Keyword Clustering

Group keywords by topic and intent so one page can target a cluster instead of one keyword per page.

Why cluster?

  • Stronger topical authority: one in-depth page can rank for many related terms.
  • Clear site structure: one URL per topic, with internal links between clusters.
  • Better UX: users find all related info in one place.

How to cluster

  • Export keywords from your tool and tag by intent (informational / navigational / transactional).
  • Group by theme: e.g. "skincare", "makeup", "haircare" for a beauty site.
  • Assign one primary keyword per cluster and secondary/long-tail to the same page.
  • Create one main page per cluster (pillar) and link to sub-topics (cluster content) for internal linking.
